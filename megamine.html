    UT students walk out of basketball game to support Office of Diversity

    WBIR 17 hours ago

    (WBIR - Knoxville) A group of University of Tennessee students walked out of a Vols basketball game on Saturday to support the Office of Diversity and Inclusion.

    This comes after members of the Tennessee Senate Education Committee voted unanimously Wednesday to amend Gov. Bill Haslam’s annual budget, taking approximately $5 million away from diversity initiatives across the UT system, and putting it towards rural outreach programs.

    The group of UT students wore all black and sat together in the student section. They left the game around 1:15 and gathered outside Thompson-Boling Arena.

    "UT has been doing the bare minimum for diversity and inclusion. We want more. We are expecting more. We are here," said Kristen Godfrey, a graduate student at UT.

    The students carried signs with the hashtag #UTDiversityMatters and the names of several state lawmakers who have proposed legislation that would defund the Office of Diversity and Inclusion.

    "I feel like when people ask me why is diversity important, they are asking me 'why is my existence important?' That's why we are doing this, as a form of survival. We have to be paid attention to on this campus," Godfrey said.

    Dozens of students joined the group, including Vol football players Alvin Kamara and Jalen Reeves-Maybin. Kamara tweeted a picture from the student section during the game.

    It's been a controversial year or so for the Office of Diversity and Inclusion. In 2015, it faced criticisms from some lawmakers for an online post encouraging the use of gender neutral pronouns, and another that advocated for religiously inclusive holiday parties.
